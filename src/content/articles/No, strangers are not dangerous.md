When I apprenticed at a police department, I watched the same scene many times. A well-meaning neighbor would call 911, alerting us to a dangerous man who "doesn't look like he belongs here," or to "unattended children in the park." On one occasion, it was a man fishing in a retaining pond; another, two children playing tetherball. Each time, the officers would roll up to the scene of the imagined crime, find no evidence of wrongdoing, and assuage the caller's fears. Often, the officers would have to reassure the caller that yes, they did the "right thing" by calling.

This scene plays again and again in the American theatre. We [avoid going to cities or talking to strangers](https://news.gallup.com/poll/544415/personal-safety-fears-three-decade-high.aspx), out of fear that they will hurt us. We call child protective services when we see a 6-year old [riding his scooter](https://bigthink.com/mind-behavior/the-quiet-disappearance-of-the-free-range-childhood/) alone. [45% of American women](https://news.gallup.com/poll/544415/personal-safety-fears-three-decade-high.aspx) say the fear of crime prevents them from taking walks, jogging or running alone. [44% of us worry frequently](https://news.gallup.com/poll/544415/personal-safety-fears-three-decade-high.aspx) about our home being broken into. [72% of gun owners](https://www.pewresearch.org/politics/2023/08/16/for-most-u-s-gun-owners-protection-is-the-main-reason-they-own-a-gun/) say they own a gun to protect themselves from strangers. 



The problem is that strangers don't steal children, attack young women, or murder indiscriminately. If you are abducted, abused, or murdered, it's not going to be by a stranger on the street. It will be at the hands of someone you know.  **big central argument goes here**

The Johns Hopkins page on stranger danger is emblematic of these fears. It urges parents to use a password system-- that is, ask your kids to only go home with a trusted adult who knows the established secret password. The problem is that adults known to children... are the ones who are likely to hurt your kid!

This fear isn't causally reducing crime, either. This is the most common rebuttal I hear, and it makes sense! The story goes that women have learned to fear men, they take precautions, those precautions cause crime to fall, therefore the fear is productive. The problem is that it *isn't true*. 

Instead of allowing children to walk to school, we drive them. Car accidents kill 1,000 to 1,200 children a year, and are the leading cause of deaths in children. We expose kids to **far more risk** when we drive them.

A Black mother who couldn't afford childcare sent her 9-year old daughter to play at the park during her shift. She was arrested. She briefly lost custody of her child. There were 3.5 million child welfare investigations last year. [More than half](https://childwelfaremonitor.org/2023/04/30/what-is-the-cause-of-racial-disparity-in-child-welfare/) of Black children will be the subject of a CPS investigation before they turn 18.

Gun owners purchase a gun for self defense-- and are then **three times likelier** to commit suicide than similar non-gun owners.
