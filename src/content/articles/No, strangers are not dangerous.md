When I apprenticed at a police department, I watched the same scene play many times. A well-meaning neighbor would call 911, alerting us to a dangerous man who "doesn't look like he belongs here," or to "unattended children in the park." On one occasion, it was a man fishing in a retaining pond; another, two children playing tetherball. Each time, the officers would roll up to the scene of the imagined crime, find no evidence of wrongdoing, and assuage the caller's fears. Often, the officers had to reassure the caller that yes, they did the "right thing" by calling.

This scene plays again and again in the American theatre. We [avoid going to cities or talking to strangers](https://news.gallup.com/poll/544415/personal-safety-fears-three-decade-high.aspx), out of fear that strangers will hurt us. We call child protective services when we see a 6-year old [riding his scooter](https://bigthink.com/mind-behavior/the-quiet-disappearance-of-the-free-range-childhood/) alone. [45% of American women](https://news.gallup.com/poll/544415/personal-safety-fears-three-decade-high.aspx) say the fear of crime prevents them from taking walks, jogging or running alone. [44% of us worry frequently](https://news.gallup.com/poll/544415/personal-safety-fears-three-decade-high.aspx) about our home being broken into. [72% of gun owners](https://www.pewresearch.org/politics/2023/08/16/for-most-u-s-gun-owners-protection-is-the-main-reason-they-own-a-gun/) say they own a gun to protect themselves from strangers.

The fear doesn't track reality. Strangers don't steal children, attack young women, or murder indiscriminately. If you are abducted, abused, or murdered, it's not going to be by a stranger on the street. It will be at the hands of someone you know. **big central data goes here**

_Homicide._ The FBI's Supplementary Homicide Reports and BJS data consistently show that in cases where the victim-offender relationship is known, the majority of homicides involve people who knew each other. consider gang violence as well.

_Child abuse._ This is the cleanest case. Department of Justice data and the National Crime Victimization Survey both show that something like 90%+ of child abuse is committed by someone the child knows — family members, family friends, coaches, clergy, etc. Stranger-perpetrated CSA is rare. This is well-established. Cite Finkelhor's work or the DOJ's "" Assault of Young Children as Reported to Law Enforcement" report.

_Sexual assault of adults._ RAINN aggregates this well — roughly 8 in 10 rapes are committed by someone known to the victim. Bureau of Justice Statistics data backs this up. The "stranger jumping out of the bushes" archetype is statistically rare; acquaintance and intimate partner assault dominate.

_Child homicide._ Roughly 70-80% of child murder victims are killed by a parent or family member, depending on age group. This skews very heavily toward family for young children and shifts somewhat for adolescents. CDC and BJS data.

_Child abduction._ The NCMEC stereotypical-kidnapping figure (stranger, weapon, ransom or harm) is around 100-115 per year in the U.S. Family abductions vastly outnumber stranger abductions.

Johns Hopkins' stranger danger warning page is a microcosm of the risk-illiterate worldview. It urges parents to use a password system-- that is, ask your kids to only go home with a trusted adult who knows the established secret password. But adults known to the family *are the ones who are likely to hurt your kid!*

Gallup polls on public safety fears don't correlate with crime rates at all. Both fear _and_ crime increased sharply in 2020.

---

Roughly [1,500 Americans died after 9/11](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2011/sep/05/september-11-road-deaths) when they chose to drive instead of fly-- driving is _orders of magnitude_ more dangerous than flying, and substituting drives for flights increases risk, a lot. Instead of allowing our children to walk to school, we drive them. Car accidents kill 1,000 to 1,200 children a year, and are the leading cause of deaths in children. We expose  children to much more risk by putting them in a Chevy Suburban instead of letting them walk or take public transit.

A Black mother who couldn't afford childcare sent her 9-year old daughter to play at the park during her shift. She was arrested. She briefly lost custody of her child. She was not alone in this indignity. There were 3.5 million child welfare investigations last year. [More than half](https://childwelfaremonitor.org/2023/04/30/what-is-the-cause-of-racial-disparity-in-child-welfare/) of Black children will be the subject of a CPS investigation before they turn 18.

Gun owners purchase a gun for self defense-- and are **several times likelier** to commit suicide than similar non-gun owners.

No one person deserves to be blamed for caring about their neighbor, their daughter, their family. But safetyism is killing us.
